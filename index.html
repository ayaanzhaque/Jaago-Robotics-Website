Jaago Robotics

Partnered with the Jaago Foundation to teach robotics and coding to the brightest students in bangladesh.

What We do

Providing Robotics Education

We teach Robotics, Coding, and STEM to underpriveledged students in Bangladesh at the Jaago Foundation. As a branch of the Jaago Foundation, we use the Lego Mindstorms EV3 Robotics kit to help young students learn introductory robotics concepts. We want to provide new opportunities for students who don't have access to them.

About The Program

Our goal is to expand Robotics and Coding in Bangladesh.

We go once every year to teach Robotics and Coding to the bright students at the Jaago Schoool in Dhaka. We have gone twice so far, in the summer of 2018 and winter of 2019.

Promoting Robotics and Skills

We aim to teach the fundamentals of Robotics and Coding to students who have no exposure. As a branch of Jaago, we want to teach motivated and inspired students who want to have new opportunites in often unaccessible fields, such as Computer Science and Engineering.

The exposure given to the students by us will open them up to new fields which will open opportunities which they would not have had before.

Fostering Education and Opportunities

We work with teachers, students, and organizers to provide the best education and new resources to students.

As each year progresses we improve our curriculum so the students will learn the most in the time we have. Each year, not only do we continue to work with previous students, we work with a new batch of students to extend our reach.

Projects

The Projects and Achievements of the Students

The Jaago students learned very quickly and achieve their goals and tasks within our limited 2 week sessions.

Levi's Presentation

In between our first and second group of students, the first group gave a presentation to Levi's, the denim company, in the hopes of receiving funding for their school. The students showed such a strong understanding of the material that they were given the grant and funds for their school.

Line Follower

For the final project, the students learned to code and implement a line follower robot, which followed a black line. Students used their new-found coding and engineering skills to create their robot. After lots of testing, all groups were able to achieve the task.

Board Presentation

For the last session with our groups the students gave a presentation to the JAAGO Board explaining the work they have done in the past two weeks, and why it matters. This was a huge success as the students were able to do this without the help of us, or their teacher.

Continued Achievement

Even though our in-person sessions are limited, the students wanted to continue to learn new concepts. Therefore, with the help of faculity, we were able to establish weekly meetings where we would send lesson plans and long term projects. The next project they began was a color sorting robot.
